Rotation Tool

- The Rotation Tool (W) is fairly easy to understand. It simply spins any object you load into your composition, clockwise or counter-clockwise.

- Use it with some keyframes to create animations that require a smooth rotation.

Pan Behind

- Use this tool (Y) to grab and move the anchor point of an element in your composition.

- The anchor point is the point in which all transformations are manipulated from.

Shape Tool

- The Shape Tool (Q) is used to create a shape within your composition.

- Click and drag to create the shape at any size and dimension you want. Hold shift during this to keep the dimensions proportionate.

- Use the drop down to select from a variety of different shapes.

Pen Tool

- The Pen Tool (G) is used to create custom paths and shapes

- This tool will create points along its path that you can select and animate afterwards.

- The drop down for this will give you various tools to make a more tailored animation.

Text Tool

- The Text Tool (CMD/CNTRL + T) simply adds a text box to your composition.

- Click anywhere in the composition to add the box, then style your text how you want in the Character Panel.

Paint Brush

- The Paint Brush (CMD/CNTRL + B) is used to draw custom designs on to a layer.

- Keep in mind that in order to use the Paint Brush, you need to open up the layer panel first (double click on the layer you want to paint on).

Clone Stamp

- The Clone Stamp (CMD/CNTRL + B) allows you to copy parts of an image from one area to another.

- This tool is only useable in the layer panel as well.

- Hold Alt/Option and click on the area that you want to copy, then click and drag over the area you want to replace.

Eraser Tool

- The Eraser (CMD/CNTRL + B) is easy to understand. In the layers panel, it can rub out parts of the layer that you do not want to be there.

- You can adjust the size and hardness of the Eraser in the Brushes Panel

Roto Brush Tool

- The Roto Brush (Option/Alt + ) is a slightly more advanced tool. It is used to isolate objects within a composition so that they can be easily manipulated. It's like a manual green screen

- You can use it to cut out undesired areas, or to select an area that you want to add specific effects to. There are a huge variety of ways in which this tool can be used.

- Use it by painting over the area of your composition that you want to manipulate, it will try to automatically detect the area you want. Hold Shift to and paint more to add to the selction, or use Alt/Option to remove areas you dont want.

- Press SpaceBar and After Effects will track the object through each frame in your composition.

- With this element isolated, you can then apply any effects you like it.

Puppet Position Pin

- The Puppet tool lets you add “pins” in the image, then move and warp the image based on where you move the pins.

- You can then use these pins in conjuction with keyframes to get some well tailored and specific movement within your animations.
